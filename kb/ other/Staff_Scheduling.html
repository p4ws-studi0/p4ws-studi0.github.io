Staff Scheduling

Operating Procedure

 

This procedure pertains to the senior staff member(s) who are creating the schedule but may be useful to other staff members to know how we create the schedule.

 

The number of staff scheduled is based on how many pets we have in-house. We predict our future occupancy based on current reservations and last year's occupancy. It is best to be slightly over-scheduled and send someone home early or call them out in advance. The Occupied Runs List for the week will be reviewed against the schedule to be sure we are staffing appropriately and must be printed the day before and tasks assigned to double-check that the staffing is adequate.

 

The schedule must be posted at least two weeks in advance. When making the schedule, keep in mind:

  • We want to have a good mix of experienced staff to train new staff. Avoid having two inexperienced staff members on at the same time performing a similar task. Schedule a more experienced bather with a less experienced, more experienced receptionist with a less experienced receptionist, etc. We aim to fully leverage our experienced staff to help accelerate the development of our less experienced team members.

  • Avoid overtime unless it is absolutely necessary. When approving a pick-up or swap, verify that the employee(s) will not incur overtime.

  • Do not count staff members in training. Always schedule trainees with a senior staff member.

  • When we are not busy, we typically schedule 6 in the morning and 4 in the evening. As we get busier, we want to add extra pet care technicians for up to 13 in the morning and 11 in the evening. Add an extra bather on high departure dates.

Every Shift Should Include the Following:

  • Manager = Oversees all operations to ensure staffing efficiency and operational accuracy. 
  • Receptionist 1 = Primary receptionist; oversees all activity at the front desk to ensure accuracy and excellent customer service. 
  • Receptionist 2 = Assists R1 and, when not occupied at the front desk, can help in the back. However, they must remain in a position where they can oversee the front desk in case their support is needed.
  • Reservation Specialist [No RS on Sunday] = Handles new customer calls, books reservations, and can assist with front desk duties, cleaning, and other tasks as needed.
  • Shift Lead 1 = Senior shift lead; supervises pet care department and is responsible for coordinating tasks such as training, assigning areas, playtimes, and cleaning.
  • Shift Lead 2 = Assists shift lead 1; supervises pet care department.
  • Pet Care Technicians = Handles cleaning, walks, playtimes, and other pet-related tasks. One must be available to cover group breaks. 
     

Every A.M. Shift Should Also Include: 

 

  • Bather = Helps with morning cleaning unless there are a large number of baths. After completing the baths, assists the pet care team as needed.
  • Group Play Attendants = Assists with morning cleaning, creates playlists, conducts new dog evaluations, monitors playgroups, and cleans the play gym and outdoor areas.

 

All positions will start at 6:45 unless otherwise noted. We have found that having two PCT come in at 5:30 to get an early start of cleaning helps with the daycare rush at 7 a.m. If we are not busy we may have the Group Play 

 

P.M. Schedule =

Every p.m. schedule will usually have one R1, R2, and two Pet Care Technician. During very slow times we may be fine with only one Pet Care Technician. Staff start time should be staggered so they are able to complete all duties with ease. R1 & R2 will be scheduled from 1:30 p.m – 8 p.m. R2 will do the rotations at 2:30, bring the dogs in from play at 3 p.m. and cats. Pet Care Technician 3:30 (hand out feeds), If there is another pet care technician, they can be scheduled for 4:30 to get there in time for walks, etc. R2, and the pet care technicians will do any left-over play times, clean west, etc.

R1 (1:30 pm – 8 pm) = responsible for everything that goes on at the front desk

R2 (1:30 pm – 8 pm) = helps R1 as needed, otherwise works in back. R2 will do the rotations at 2:30, bring the dogs in from play at 3 p.m. and cats.

Pet Care Technicians (1:30-3:30 pm – 8 pm) = hand out feeds, walks, closing tasks etc.

During busy times we will have up to 6 staff members in the evening. Ramp up as needed.

Manager/Supervisor may send staff home early if we are slow.

Things to Keep in Mind When Creating the Schedule

  • Staffing is our biggest expense. We do not want to be overstaffed. We find that with a lean staff everyone is more efficient.

  • Mondays can be busy with departures from the weekends. We may need to schedule an extra person in the morning on Mondays.

  • Fridays are usually very busy with arrivals. We should schedule someone extra in the evening (and sometimes in the morning as well) to complete all of the playtimes for dogs coming in later in the day.

  • Saturdays and Sundays don’t usually need an extra person as we open later and close earlier

  • Keep in mind things going on at the high school such as prom, sports seasons, etc. and plan ahead.

  • Be fair when scheduling:

  • Everyone needs to work holidays. If they request a holiday off, make sure they have or are on for other holidays.

  • High school students should each have the same number of shifts if possible.

  • Try to keep schedules the same from week to week if possible. If you need to put someone on for a day they usually don’t work, ask first.

  • Everyone works weekends. If someone is not available to work weekends on a regular basis, they may be taken off the schedule all together.
