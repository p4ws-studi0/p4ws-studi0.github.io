Pet Handling Basics

Operating Procedure

 

This document will outline the basics of good dog and cat handling. Safety comes 1st to ensure pets are happy and healthy when staying with us! 

 

  • Always treat pets with the utmost respect and care.
  • Always keep in mind customer perception, there may be a tour walking through. You should ALWAYS be kind and respectful to all animals in our care.
  • Never raise your voice towards a pet, unless it is absolutely necessary to keep them safe.
  • NEVER put your face near a pet's mouth.
  • If a pet is "experienced staff only" (it will say this on the run card, and in the notes) do not handle them unless granted permission from a supervisor or manager.
  • As outlined in PackPro, keep the leash high up on the dog's neck, keep a short leash, walking the dog to the left of you, and using BOTH HANDS on the leash to maintain control.
  • Do not let dogs interact when on the leash, as some dogs can be unexpectedly reactive on a leash.
  • No handling dogs if you are the only person in the building.
  • Do not take pets through the lobby when bringing them to their suite.
  • Never leave animals from different families unattended. 
  • If there is anything unusual about a pet, read their notes, make a new note if necessary, and/or reach out to a supervisor or manager for assistance.
  • Poop should be picked up as soon as you see it, even if it is not from your dog.
  • Discourage indoor elimination. Watch for signs they need to go (sniffing, etc.) let them out before they go inside.
